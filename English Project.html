Exploring the life and work of Ila Sachani

Ila Sachani is known for her vigrant embroidery creation. She learned embroidery using feet instead of hands and is a very unique and creative artist. At first she faced many challenges, but with family support and lots of determination she overcame many challenges and has become an extraordinary artist.

Ila Sachani was born in Moti Vavdi village in Bhavnagar, Gujarat in a farmer's family. From the very start, Ila's life was not easy because she was born with a condition that made her hands hang loosely by her hands. This meant that, unlike other children, she could not use her hands in the same way. While other kids drew pictures in the sand or picked flower, Ila often wondered why she could not do the same things. She wanted to hold a piece of chalk like the other children and draw pictures on the slate, but she could not. Despite being born with this type of condition, Ila's mother and grandmother, skilled in Kathaiwar embroidery, taught her to embroider using her feet. With determination, Ila mastered threading needles and learned various embroidery styles beyond Gujarat.

Initially creating designs for family and friends, Ila's talent soon gained local admiration and government recognition. Her work was showcased at state exhibitions, leading to national acclaim and prestigious awards, including the President's medal.

Such a insipirational story of Ila sachani who despite her disabilities chose the harder way and became a celebrated and extraordinary artist making her life wonderful. Her embroidery, created with her feet, reminds us that art has the power to rise above phsyical boundaries and touch the soul. She proved, that challenges can be changed into victories with focus, hard work, positivity and never give up.
